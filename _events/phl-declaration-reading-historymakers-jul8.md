Beyond the big noon and 6 PM reenactments, Historic Philadelphia's costumed **History Makers** give a short daily reading of the Declaration of Independence behind Independence Hall, by the Commodore John Barry statue, at **3:45 PM** — so on the 250th anniversary you can catch the Declaration read three times in one day. Famous and not-so-famous "History Makers" recite the document and stick around for pictures. Free; runs Tuesday–Saturday, June 2 through September 7.
