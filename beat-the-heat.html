Beat the Heat

It's forecast to hit around 103°F in Philadelphia today, with a heat index near 111°F — the city is under a Heat Health Emergency. Here are the best air-conditioned, indoor things to do in Philly and the suburbs while the sun does its worst. Save the fireworks and parades for the cooler evening.

Stay safe in the heat

Philadelphia has declared a Heat Health Emergency from Wed, July 1 (11 a.m.) through Sat, July 4 (8 p.m.). Drink water, stay indoors during the hottest hours (roughly 11 a.m.–6 p.m.), check on older neighbors, and never leave kids or pets in a car.

Need help or a cool place to go? Call the free PCA Heatline at 215-765-9040 (8:30 a.m.–8:30 p.m. during the emergency) to speak with a nurse, dial 311 for the nearest cooling center, or call 215-232-1984 for 24/7 street outreach. Details: phila.gov heat health emergency ↗.
